Advanced VPC Concepts: Scaling & High Performance

Posted on April 30, 2026 • 15 min read Advanced Architecture

Taking the Network to the Next Level

Once you've mastered the basics of subnets and firewalls, enterprise-scale cloud architecture demands more complex connectivity patterns. In this post, we explore how to scale, connect, and optimize VPCs for global workloads.

1. VPC Peering: The Private Highway

VPC Peering is a direct network connection between two VPCs that allows you to route traffic between them using private IP addresses.

  • Cross-Region Peering: Peer VPCs across different AWS regions. Your data stays on the AWS global backbone.
  • The "Transitive" Trap: VPC Peering is not transitive. If A is peered with B, and B with C, A cannot talk to C through B.
  • Overlapping CIDRs: You cannot peer VPCs if their IP ranges overlap.

2. Transit Gateway: The Central Hub

As your organization grows to hundreds of VPCs, managing thousands of peering connections becomes impossible. AWS Transit Gateway (TGW) acts as a central hub-and-spoke router.

  • Connect thousands of VPCs and on-premises networks to a single gateway.
  • Supports Transitive Routing.
  • Simplifies management with a single point of control.

3. Hybrid Connectivity: VPN vs. Direct Connect

  • Site-to-Site VPN: Quick to deploy, uses the public internet (encrypted).
  • AWS Direct Connect (DX): A dedicated physical line from your data center to AWS. Consistent performance and lower latency.

4. PrivateLink & VPC Endpoints

  • Gateway Endpoints: Free endpoints for S3 and DynamoDB.
  • Interface Endpoints (PrivateLink): Reach services privately across different accounts/VPCs without touching the internet.

5. Global Acceleration

AWS Global Accelerator uses the AWS global network to route your users' traffic, improving performance by up to 60% by avoiding internet congestion.

Expert Architect Tip: The Overlap Nightmare

Overlapping CIDR blocks are a common headache during mergers. Standard Peering or TGW will fail. To resolve this, use AWS PrivateLink to expose specific services across the overlap, or implement Private NAT Gateways.
